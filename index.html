München — Arrival & Oktoberfest
3 nights · No rushing · Oktoberfest on Day 2
Hotel / night
€70–90
Motel One
Day 1
Reunion Day — No Sightseeing Required
"After an overnight flight across the Atlantic, the only thing that matters is the hug at arrivals, a long shower, and a quiet dinner somewhere warm."
Airport pickup / hotel meetupShower · Nap · CoffeeQuiet dinner near hotelNo sightseeing — jet lag is real
The Canadian arrives on an overnight flight — don't plan anything demanding. A slow walk around the Viktualienmarkt and dinner at a neighbourhood restaurant is the perfect first evening in Europe together.
Stay at
Motel One München-Parkstadt Schwabing
€70-90/night
Day 2
🍺 Oktoberfest — The Relaxed Version
"Arrive before 2pm on a weekday. Find a table inside a tent. Order two Masskrüge. The world's greatest party is better when you don't rush it."
Weekday visit before 2pm — far less crowdedOktoberfest — free entry, beer ~€15 per MassStay 3–4 hours, then leave while you can still walkEvening: romantic walk through Maxvorstadt or Haidhausen
The secret to Oktoberfest for couples: arrive early, get seats inside a tent (Hofbräu or Augustiner), order a shared Brezn and two beers, people-watch for 3 hours. Leave by 5pm before it gets raucous.
Day 3
Slow Munich — Parks, Rowboats & Beer Gardens
"Sleep in. The English Garden is the world's largest city park — bigger than Central Park. Rent a rowboat. Find a biergarten that isn't Oktoberfest. Breathe."
English Garden — morning walkRowboat on Kleinhesseloher See — €15/hrChinese Tower beer garden — free to sit, cheap beer
The rowboat on Kleinhesseloher See is genuinely one of the most romantic things in Munich. €15 for an hour, no crowds, weeping willows, ducks. Better than any restaurant view.

Day 4
Arrive & The Electric Boat
"A silent electric boat glides between cliffs that plunge two thousand metres straight into emerald water. The boatman stops mid-lake and plays an alphorn — the echo comes back from the cliff face."
Electric boat to St. Bartholomä — €20 ppMalerwinkel (Painter's Corner) viewpoint — freeEvening Bavarian dinner in Berchtesgaden town
Take the 10am or 11am boat to avoid crowds. Get off at St. Bartholomä — don't take the last boat back immediately. Sit by the church, have a coffee, let the mountains surround you for an hour.

Day 5
Jennerbahn Cable Car + Spa Afternoon
"The gondola rises to 1,800 metres above the lake. Then — warm saltwater at Watzmann Therme. Your legs thank you. Your soul thanks you."
Jennerbahn cable car — €28 ppSummit walk (30–45 min)Lunch at mountain restaurantWatzmann Therme spa — €12-15 pp
The Jennerbahn sits directly at the Königssee lakeside — you can look down at the emerald water from 1,800m. Afterward, soak in the saltwater thermal baths. This is what alpine romance feels like.

🇨🇭 Switzerland · Days 6–8
Zermatt — The Matterhorn
3 nights · Car-free village · Ultimate romance
Hotel / night
CHF 140–170
Matterhornblick
Day 6
Arrival in Zermatt — First View of the Matterhorn
"The train winds up from Visp. Then — a gap between the valley walls — and the Matterhorn appears. Sharp. Impossible. Perfect."
Travel day: Berchtesgaden → Zermatt (~6-7 hours)Evening walk through car-free villageDinner with Matterhorn view
Zermatt is car-free. Park in Täsch (CHF 15-20/day) and take the shuttle train. Most hotels offer free pickup from Zermatt station.
Stay at
Hotel Matterhornblick · Matterhorn-view room
CHF 140-170/night
Day 7
Gornergrat — The Classic Matterhorn View
"The cogwheel train climbs to 3,089 metres. The Matterhorn stands alone. You hike down to Riffelsee and see it reflected in the water."
Gornergrat Railway — CHF 80 pp with Half-Fare CardHike down to Riffelsee (1-1.5 hours)Lunch at Riffelberg with Matterhorn view
The hike from Gornergrat to Riffelsee is gentle downhill. The lake reflects the Matterhorn perfectly on calm days. Go in the morning for the best light.
Day 8
Do Nothing Day — Spa, Balcony, Matterhorn
"No alarms. No plans. Coffee on the balcony watching the Matterhorn change color. Then an afternoon soak in the thermal baths. This is the most romantic day of the trip."
Morning: Sleep in, café breakfast, Matterhorn-view balconyAfternoon: Public thermal baths — CHF 30-40 ppHorse-drawn carriage at sunset — CHF 50-70Fondue dinner for two
The "do nothing" day is the secret to real romance. No rushing. No checklist. Just the two of you, the mountain, and warm water. This is what you'll remember years from now.

Arrive Paris — Eiffel Tower Sparkle
"The TGV out of Basel accelerates to 300 km/h. Then — after 5.5 hours — the train slows, and suddenly the Eiffel Tower is outside your window."
Travel: Zermatt → Paris (~5.5 hours)Evening: Eiffel Tower sparkle from Trocadéro — freeFirst Paris dinner — crêpes on a bench near the river

Day 10
Slow Paris — Champagne Picnic & Seine
"Sleep until 9. Find a corner café. Order café au lait and a croissant. Then buy a baguette, brie, and a bottle of Crémant. Lay on the Champ de Mars grass. Watch the Tower sparkle at 10pm."
Pont Neuf → Notre-Dame Seine walkChampagne picnic under the Eiffel Tower — €25 totalEiffel Tower sparkle at 10pm — free
Buy a bottle of Crémant de Bourgogne (French sparkling wine, nearly as good as Champagne, half the price) from Monoprix. Add a baguette, brie, and macarons. This picnic costs €25 and is more romantic than any restaurant.
Day 11
Montmartre at Dawn & Seine Dinner Cruise
"Set one alarm — just one — for 6:30am. Walk up to Sacré-Cœur as the sun rises over Paris. The city below is rose-gold and almost silent."
Sacré-Cœur sunrise — freePlace du Tertre artists' squareSeine river dinner cruise — €110-150 pp (splurge)
The Seine dinner cruise is your big splurge. Book a window table at sunset. You'll float past Notre-Dame, the Louvre, and under Pont Alexandre III while the Eiffel Tower sparkles.

Day 13
Rijksmuseum & Hortus Botanicus
"Whisper to each other in the famous Rijksmuseum library. Then lose yourselves in the tropical greenhouses of the Hortus — butterflies, giant lily pads, and a silence that feels like a secret."
Rijksmuseum — free with I amsterdam CardHortus Botanicus — €13.50 or free with cardMagere Brug (Skinny Bridge) at night — make a wish together
The Rijksmuseum library is one of the most romantic spots in Amsterdam — quiet, ancient, and perfect for a whispered conversation. The Hortus gardens are a peaceful escape from the city.
Day 14
Zaanse Schans — Windmills & Dutch Cheese
"A 17-minute train takes you to a village of working windmills, wooden shoe makers, and cheese farms. It's like stepping into a Dutch painting."
Train to Zaanse Schans — €7-10 pp returnWindmill walk, cheese tasting, wooden shoe demoFinal dinner at Cafe Bern (Nieuwmarkt) — book ahead
Zaanse Schans is touristy but wonderful. Go early (before 10am) to avoid crowds. The cheese tasting is free and surprisingly generous.
Day 15
Last Morning — Coffee & Goodbye
"No agenda. A final coffee at Latei on Zeedijk — the cutest interior and best coffee in the city. Then the train to Schiphol, carrying two weeks of memories."
Final Amsterdam breakfast — stroopwafels & coffeeMorning walk — no map, no planTrain to Schiphol Airport — 15-20 min, allow 2hrs before flight
Walk Amsterdam without a destination on your last morning. The canals are most beautiful when you're not trying to see anything. Follow a street that looks interesting. Find a courtyard. This is how you say goodbye.
