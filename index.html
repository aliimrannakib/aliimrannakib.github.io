Day 1
Reunion Day — No Sightseeing Required
"After an overnight flight across the Atlantic, the only thing that matters is the hug at arrivals, a long shower, and a quiet dinner somewhere warm."
Airport pickup / hotel meetupShower · Nap · CoffeeQuiet dinner near hotelNo sightseeing — jet lag is real
The Canadian arrives on an overnight flight — don't plan anything demanding. A slow walk around the Viktualienmarkt and dinner at a neighbourhood restaurant is the perfect first evening in Europe together.

Motel One München-Parkstadt Schwabing · €70-90/night
Day 2
🍺 Oktoberfest — The Relaxed Version
"Arrive before 2pm on a weekday. Find a table inside a tent. Order two Masskrüge. The world's greatest party is better when you don't rush it."
Weekday visit before 2pm — far less crowdedOktoberfest — free entry, beer ~€14–16 per MassStay 3–4 hours, then leave while you can still walkEvening: romantic walk through Maxvorstadt or Haidhausen
The secret to Oktoberfest for couples: arrive early, get seats inside a tent (Hofbräu or Augustiner), order a shared Brezn and two beers, people-watch for 3 hours. Leave by 5pm before it gets raucous. The evening neighbourhood walk is the real romance.

Same hotel · 2nd night
Day 3
Slow Munich — Parks, Rowboats & Beer Gardens
"Sleep in. The English Garden is the world's largest city park — bigger than Central Park. Rent a rowboat. Find a biergarten that isn't Oktoberfest. Breathe."
English Garden — morning walkRowboat on Kleinhesseloher See — €15/hrChinese Tower beer garden — free to sit, cheap beer
The rowboat on Kleinhesseloher See is genuinely one of the most romantic things in Munich. €15 for an hour, no crowds, weeping willows, ducks. Better than any restaurant view.

Day 4
Arrive & The Electric Boat
"A silent electric boat glides between cliffs that plunge two thousand metres straight into emerald water. The boatman stops mid-lake and plays an alphorn — the echo comes back from the cliff face."
Electric boat to St. Bartholomä — €20 ppMalerwinkel (Painter's Corner) viewpoint — freeEvening Bavarian dinner in Berchtesgaden town
Take the 10am or 11am boat to avoid crowds. Get off at St. Bartholomä — don't take the last boat back immediately. Sit by the church, have a coffee, let the mountains surround you for an hour.

Day 5
Jennerbahn Cable Car — Above the World
"The gondola rises to 1,800 metres above the lake. The Königssee shrinks to a mirror below. The Alps stretch in every direction. You eat lunch at a mountain restaurant with your feet dangling over Bavaria."
Jennerbahn cable car — ~€28 pp returnSummit walk (30–45 min gentle trail)Lunch at mountain restaurant — €15–20 pp
The Jennerbahn sits directly at the Königssee lakeside — you can look down at the emerald water from 1,800m. On a clear day you see Austria, Germany and what feels like all of creation. This is the highlight of the Königssee stay.

3 nights · Car-free village · Ultimate romance
Hotel / night
CHF 140–170

Day 6
Arrival in Zermatt — First View of the Matterhorn
"The train winds up from Visp. Then — a gap between the valley walls — and the Matterhorn appears. Sharp. Impossible. Perfect."
Travel day: Berchtesgaden → Zermatt (~6-7 hours)Evening walk through car-free villageDinner with Matterhorn view
Zermatt is car-free. Park in Täsch (CHF 15-20/day) and take the shuttle train. Most hotels offer free pickup from Zermatt station between 8:00-19:00. [citation:7]
Stay at
Hotel Matterhornblick · Matterhorn-view room · CHF 140-170/night [citation:1]
Day 7
Gornergrat — The Classic Matterhorn View
"The cogwheel train climbs to 3,089 metres. The Matterhorn stands alone. You hike down to Riffelsee and see it reflected in the water — the most famous mountain photograph in the world, and it's yours."
Gornergrat Railway — CHF 80 pp with Half-Fare CardHike down to Riffelsee (1-1.5 hours)Lunch at Riffelberg with Matterhorn view
The hike from Gornergrat to Riffelsee is gentle downhill. The lake reflects the Matterhorn perfectly on calm days. Go in the morning for the best light.
Day 8
Matterhorn Glacier Paradise — Standing Inside a Glacier
"The highest cable car in Europe takes you to 3,883 metres. Then you walk inside a glacier — ice caves, sculptures, a frozen slide. The view from the top looks into Italy, France, and Switzerland."
Glacier Paradise + Ice Palace — CHF 80 pp with Half-Fare CardHorse-drawn carriage ride at sunset — CHF 50-70Fondue dinner for two
The horse-drawn carriage ride through Zermatt at sunset is pure romance. Blankets provided. The clip-clop of hooves on cobblestones with the Matterhorn turning pink — worth every franc.

Day 12
Slow Paris — Seine Walk & Champagne Picnic
"Sleep until 9. Find a corner café. Order café au lait and a croissant. Then buy a baguette, brie, and a bottle of Crémant. Lay on the Champ de Mars grass. Watch the Tower sparkle at 10pm."
Pont Neuf → Notre-Dame Seine walkChampagne picnic under the Eiffel Tower — €25 totalEiffel Tower sparkle at 10pm — free from below
Buy a bottle of Crémant de Bourgogne (French sparkling wine, nearly as good as Champagne, half the price) from Monoprix. Add a baguette, brie, and macarons. This picnic costs €25 and is more romantic than any restaurant.
Day 13
Montmartre at Dawn & Seine Dinner Cruise
"Set one alarm — just one — for 6:30am. Walk up to Sacré-Cœur as the sun rises over Paris. The city below is rose-gold and almost silent. Then tonight: dinner on the Seine."
Sacré-Cœur sunrise — freePlace du Tertre artists' squareSeine river dinner cruise — €110-150 pp
The Seine dinner cruise is your big splurge. Book a window table at sunset. You'll float past Notre-Dame, the Louvre, and under Pont Alexandre III while the Eiffel Tower sparkles. Worth every euro.
Day 14
Versailles & A Last Grand Evening
"The gardens of the Sun King. The Grand Canal stretching to the horizon. Fountains erupting. Then back to Paris for one final dinner in a candlelit bistro."
Versailles Palace — €20 pp · book online aheadVersailles gardens — free on footFinal dinner: Saint-Germain bistro
The Versailles gardens are free to walk through. Skip the expensive fountain shows and instead walk the Grand Canal at 5pm when the crowds thin and the light turns golden.
Day 15
Last Morning in Paris — Au Revoir
"No agenda. A final coffee at the same corner café. A slow walk wherever Paris takes you. Then the RER B to CDG — and the long flight home carrying two weeks of memories."
Final Paris breakfast — croissant & café crèmeMorning walk — no map, no planRER B to CDG — allow 45min + 2hrs before flight
Walk Paris without a destination on your last morning. The city is most beautiful when you're not trying to see anything. Follow a street that looks interesting. Find a courtyard. Buy a last baguette.

"The most romantic journeys are never rushed — they're savoured, one slow morning at a time."
