✦ How to Get Between Cities ✦
Eurail Pass vs Point-to-Point — Honest Comparison

🎫 With Eurail Pass

Flexibility · Zero Planning Stress
  • Eurail Global 5-day Flex — covers all major legs
  • Germany & Switzerland: hop on any train, no reservation fee
  • Paris TGV: compulsory reservation ~€12 pp on top
  • Gornergrat & Swiss mountain railways: 25% discount
  • Use Bayern Ticket for Bavaria — save your flex days
  • Best if you want to change plans spontaneously
€636 for 2 people · 2nd class · 5-day flex
Worth it if you want zero planning. The 25% Gornergrat discount saves CHF ~30 for 2 — partially offsets cost.

🎟 Point-to-Point (Recommended)

Cheapest · Book 4–6 Weeks Ahead
  • Bayern Ticket (Munich + Berchtesgaden legs): €44 for 2
  • Berchtesgaden → Zermatt via Zurich (ÖBB + SBB advance): ~€90–120 for 2
  • Zermatt → Lucerne (SBB advance, book on sbb.ch): ~€50–80 for 2
  • Lucerne → Paris TGV Lyria (book on trainline.com): ~€80–140 for 2
  • Swiss Half-Fare Card covers 50% off all Swiss legs
  • Total transport ~€400–500 for 2 · saves ~€140 vs Eurail
~€450 for 2 people · all legs booked 4–6 wks ahead
Best strategy: Bayern Ticket for Bavaria, advance P2P for long legs, Swiss Half-Fare for Switzerland. Skip Eurail entirely and save €140–200 for the couple.
